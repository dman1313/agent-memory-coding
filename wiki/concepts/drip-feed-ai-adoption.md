# Drip-feed AI Adoption

## Definition

A change management strategy for introducing AI tools to hesitant staff: start slowly, introduce tools incrementally ("drip-feed"), so that by the time full adoption is required, staff are already familiar and on board. Coined in the context of school and nonprofit leadership.

## Key Characteristics

- Reduces resistance at the critical moment of full deployment
- Works with the reality that change takes time and people need exposure
- Contrasted with "waiting for AI to settle down" — which is not a viable strategy
- Especially relevant for education and nonprofit contexts where tech adoption is uneven

## Applications

- School leaders introducing email triage agents to teachers
- Principals building familiarity before mandating agentic workflows
- Any org where staff are hesitant about AI
