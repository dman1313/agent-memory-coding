Summary:
The meeting covered the review and improvement of report cards, assessment practices, and teacher training in the PYP curriculum. Key points included the need for consistency across subjects, providing actionable advice to parents, and improving transparency in assessment methods.

Topics and Discussion Points:
• Report Card Review
  - Different examples from various schools were examined
  - Need to remove redundant information
  - Importance of providing diverse samples for reference

• Assessment of Learner Profile and ATLs
  - Challenges in assessing attributes like "caring"
  - Various methods used by teachers (notebooks, charts)
  - Debate on using terms like "excelling" for certain attributes

• Teacher Training and Experience
  - Some teachers are highly organized with rubrics and checklists
  - Others lack experience but are improving
  - Challenges with new teachers unfamiliar with PYP concepts

• Systematic Approach to Assessment
  - Goal of standardizing assessment practices across subjects
  - Recognition that this is a gradual process

Decisions:
1. Review and potentially revise the grading scale for Learner Profile attributes
2. Continue providing training and support for teachers, especially those new to PYP
3. Work with Neha to finalize the report card design
4. Focus on improving consistency, actionable advice, and transparency in assessments
5. Gradually implement a more standardized approach to assessment across all subjects
