## Others

Based on the discussion in the note, some key next steps for the literacy curriculum could be:

1. Map out text types and writing genres for each grade level (1-5)

2. Align mapped text types/genres with Ontario standards 

3. Develop a consistent framework for teaching writing across grades, potentially using the writing cycle process

4. Create exemplars/samples of low, medium, and high quality writing for each text type and grade level

5. Compile a set of graphic organizers and templates teachers can use for different text types

6. Identify mentor texts to use as models for each text type/genre

7. Develop more guidance and lesson plans for new teachers who need additional support

8. Build out the reading curriculum, including strategies for teaching comprehension skills

9. Address gaps in available leveled reading materials, especially for genres like historical fiction

10. Consider implementing reading challenges or programs to encourage more independent reading

11. Evaluate current resources like Raz Kids and determine if additional online reading platforms are needed

The overall goal seems to be creating more structure and consistency in the literacy program, while still allowing flexibility for teachers to adapt to their students' needs and the PYP framework. Taking a phased approach and focusing on writing first before tackling reading appears to be the current priority.
