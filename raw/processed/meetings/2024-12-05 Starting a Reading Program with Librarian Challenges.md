# Starting a Reading Program with Librarian Challenges

Date: 2024-12-05

## Summary

Efforts are being made to develop a reading and writing program, with a strong emphasis on involving a teacher-librarian to manage resources like novels for classroom use. The librarian's role is crucial for coordinating literacy programs and aligning reading content with classroom literacy activities. Budget constraints have led to difficulties in acquiring resources, prompting individuals to purchase books personally. There is a need for structured understanding and availability of resources for a literacy program to function effectively.

A writing process model is proposed to ensure consistent teaching practices across all classes, with an emphasis on modeling the writing process for students and involving parents in the cycle. Discussions highlight the importance of aligning writing programs with Ontario standards and emphasizing key genres like narrative, nonfiction, and poetry at different levels to improve student writing skills within the PYP curriculum framework.

Professional development days in January will be utilized to establish this structured writing program, integrating consistent literacy practices throughout the school. Workshops will address the writing cycle, curriculum mapping, and evaluation processes. There is also a focus on documenting and aligning early childhood reading activities with the PYP requirements, ensuring all stages of literacy development are recorded and accessible. Collaboration with consultants is planned to leverage experience and improve the literacy framework within the school.
