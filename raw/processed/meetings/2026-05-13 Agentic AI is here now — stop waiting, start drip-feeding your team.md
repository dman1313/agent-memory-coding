# Agentic AI is here now — stop waiting, start drip-feeding your team

Date: 2026-05-13

## Meeting Report

### NVIDIA Keynote & the "OpenClaw" Question

- Jensen's keynote prompt — "What is your OpenClaw solution?" — reframed as a broader challenge: how will your organization leverage agentic AI frameworks to support operations
- The core question posed to leaders: are you actively stepping into AI adoption, or waiting on the sidelines

### What Agentic AI Means

- Agentic AI goes beyond typing prompts into a chatbot — it refers to AI systems that go off and perform work autonomously on your behalf
- OpenAI and Claude Code are both developing office-oriented agentic solutions
- The speaker identifies as an agentic engineer, actively working with these agents and observing rapid week-to-week changes in the space
- Considered waiting for the technology to stabilize, but concluded that waiting for it to calm down or assuming it will go away is not a viable strategy

### Change Management & Early Adoption Strategy

- Recommended approach: drip-feed AI tools gradually to hesitant staff now, so that by the time full adoption is necessary, those people are already on board
- Early adoption builds internal familiarity and reduces resistance at the point of critical need

### Practical Use Cases

- Education and leadership example: using agents to filter and surface priority parent emails, reducing time spent on busy work and freeing up more face-to-face time with teachers and staff
- Pizzeria example illustrating domain-specific agentic workflows:
  - Operator uses voice notes to feed the agent contextual knowledge — busy Fridays, snowy weather patterns, delivery zones that take longer
  - Agent collates that information and adjusts scheduling and customer communication automatically
  - Example output: when a delivery to Airport Hill is placed on December 27th in North Bay, Ontario during a snowstorm, the agent flags the delay and prompts a proactive customer notification with a fluctuating 30-minute buffer communicated upfront
- Education admissions example:
  - Agent connected to school handbooks, newsletters, and program information
  - Functions as a personalized, conversational interface for prospective families — not a generic chatbot
  - Can answer specific questions and represent what the school is actually doing in real terms

### The Shift in Marketing & Communication

- Current marketing paradigm — polished Instagram photos, curated perfection — is expected to become less relevant in the AI generation
- Anticipated paradigm shift: audiences will move toward valuing authenticity, real use-case scenarios, testimonials, and direct Q&A interactions
- AI agents can facilitate this by enabling personalized, responsive engagement with prospective clients or families rather than one-way broadcast marketing

### The Broader Challenge to Leaders

- The central question remains: what is your agentic solution
- The evolution is not just about using a chatbot for occasional tasks — it is about building an office of agents with different skill sets that reduce workload and enhance performance as a researcher, teacher, leader, or operator
